Dell Technologies is a major American multinational technology company that provides a broad range of hardware, software, and IT services globally. It was founded by Michael Dell in 1984, who currently serves as the company's chairman and CEO. 
Company Overview
Dell Technologies operates under two main divisions: the Client Solutions Group (CSG) and the Infrastructure Solutions Group (ISG). 
Client Solutions Group (CSG): Focuses on developing and selling desktop PCs, notebooks (such as the Dell Inspiron and Alienware gaming laptops), tablets, and peripherals like monitors and printers.
Infrastructure Solutions Group (ISG): Provides enterprise-level solutions including servers, data storage devices, and networking equipment, as well as associated services and software. 
The company serves a wide range of clients, from individual consumers and small and medium-sized businesses to large enterprises and governmental agencies. 
Key Business Strategy & Mission
Dell's core business strategy, pioneered by Michael Dell, involves selling systems directly to customers, which allows for customization and efficient supply chain management. 
Mission: Dell Technologies' mission is "to create technology that empowers people and enriches their lives".
Vision: Its vision is "to empower countries, communities, customers, and people everywhere to use technology so that they can thrive".
Innovation Focus: The company heavily invests in research and development, particularly in emerging areas like Artificial Intelligence (AI), hybrid cloud, and edge computing solutions.
